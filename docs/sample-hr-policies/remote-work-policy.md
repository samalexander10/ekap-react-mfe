# Remote Work Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for employees working remotely, ensuring consistent standards for productivity, security, communication, and well-being regardless of work location.

## 2. Eligibility

Remote work eligibility is determined based on:
- Job function and role requirements
- Performance record (must be in good standing)
- Tenure (minimum 90 days with the company)
- Manager and HR approval

Not all roles are eligible for remote work. Roles requiring physical presence, specialized equipment, or in-person customer interaction may not qualify.

## 3. Work Arrangements

### 3.1 Fully Remote
- Employee works from home or approved remote location full-time
- Must be within a state where the company is registered as an employer
- International remote work requires advance approval from HR and Legal

### 3.2 Hybrid
- Combination of in-office and remote workdays
- Hybrid schedule must be agreed upon with manager
- Minimum in-office expectations vary by team and are set by department heads

### 3.3 Temporary Remote Work
- Short-term remote work for travel, illness, or personal circumstances
- Approved on a case-by-case basis by manager
- May not require formal remote work agreement for periods under 30 days

## 4. Request Process

1. Discuss remote work interest with your direct manager
2. Submit a Remote Work Request through the HR portal (hr.company.com/remote-work)
3. HR reviews for policy compliance and tax/legal considerations
4. Receive written approval before beginning remote work arrangement

Arrangements are reviewed quarterly and may be modified or revoked based on business needs or performance.

## 5. Expectations

### 5.1 Availability
- Maintain your standard working hours unless otherwise agreed
- Be responsive to messages within 2 hours during core hours (10am–3pm local time)
- Notify your manager promptly of any planned absences or schedule changes
- Maintain calendar accuracy with your work location

### 5.2 Performance
- Remote work is a privilege contingent on maintaining performance standards
- Deliverables and deadlines are not adjusted based on remote status
- Managers conduct quarterly check-ins specifically to assess remote work effectiveness

### 5.3 Meetings
- Attend all required meetings via video unless otherwise specified
- Enable your camera during team meetings unless there is a valid reason
- Maintain a professional and distraction-free environment during meetings

## 6. Technology Requirements

### 6.1 Equipment
The company provides:
- Laptop computer
- Standard peripherals (mouse, keyboard)
- Monitor (upon manager approval for remote workers)
- $500 home office setup stipend (one-time, for approved remote workers)

Employees are responsible for:
- Reliable internet connection (minimum 25 Mbps download / 10 Mbps upload)
- Professional, appropriate workspace
- Backup internet option for critical deadlines

### 6.2 Stipends
- **Internet:** $50/month reimbursement with receipt
- **Phone:** $30/month mobile stipend
- Stipends are reported as taxable income

## 7. Security Requirements

Remote employees must:
- Use company-provided VPN when accessing internal systems
- Enable full-disk encryption on all work devices
- Use multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all company systems
- Lock screen whenever stepping away from work device
- Avoid using public Wi-Fi without VPN
- Never allow family members or others to use company devices
- Report lost or stolen devices immediately to IT Security (security@company.com)

### 7.1 Home Network Security
- Use WPA2 or WPA3 encryption on home router
- Change default router passwords
- Create a separate network segment for work devices if possible

## 8. Workspace Requirements

Remote employees are responsible for maintaining a workspace that:
- Is free from distractions during working hours
- Has adequate lighting and ergonomic setup
- Complies with local building and occupancy requirements
- Protects the confidentiality of sensitive company or customer information
- Is not a public location for sensitive conversations

## 9. Expense Reimbursement

Eligible remote work expenses:
- Internet and phone stipends (see Section 6.2)
- Home office equipment approved in advance by manager
- Coworking space fees (up to $200/month with manager approval)

Expenses must be submitted through Concur within 30 days with receipts.

## 10. Health and Safety

- Employees are responsible for maintaining a safe, ergonomic workspace
- Ergonomic equipment requests can be made through IT/HR
- Work-related injuries at home may be covered by workers' compensation; report immediately to HR
- The company's duty of care extends to the remote work environment

## 11. Tax and Legal Considerations

Remote work in states or countries where the company does not have a registered presence may create tax obligations or legal complications. Employees must:
- Only work remotely from locations pre-approved by HR
- Notify HR before relocating to a different state or country
- Understand that relocation may affect compensation (depending on local cost of living benchmarks)

## 12. Returning to Office

Remote work arrangements may be modified or ended by:
- Mutual agreement between employee and manager
- Business need (with 30 days' notice when possible)
- Performance concerns
- Policy violations
