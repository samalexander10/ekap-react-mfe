# Legal Name Change Process

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources
**Review Cycle:** As needed

---

## 1. Overview

This document describes the process for employees who need to update their legal name in company systems following a legal name change event (marriage, divorce, court order, etc.).

## 2. Reasons for Name Change

Common qualifying events include:
- Marriage
- Divorce or dissolution of domestic partnership
- Court-ordered name change
- Adoption
- Gender transition
- Other legal name change

## 3. Required Documentation

To process a legal name change, you must provide ONE of the following:

| Document Type | Notes |
|---------------|-------|
| Marriage Certificate | Certified copy or government-issued |
| Divorce Decree | Must reflect new/reverted name |
| Court Order | Must be signed by a judge |
| Updated Government ID | Driver's license, passport, or state ID reflecting new name |
| Social Security Card | With new name |

Documents must be legible. Originals or certified copies are preferred; clear photographs or scans are acceptable for the digital submission process.

## 4. Submission Process

### 4.1 Self-Service Portal (Preferred)
1. Log in to the EKAP HR portal
2. Navigate to **My Profile → Legal Name Change Request**
3. Enter your current legal name and requested new name
4. Select the document type and upload your supporting document
5. Click **Submit**

You will receive an email confirmation with your Request ID within minutes of submission.

### 4.2 In-Person Submission
If you prefer or need to submit in person:
1. Schedule an appointment with your HR Business Partner
2. Bring original or certified copy of supporting documentation
3. HR will process the request and provide a receipt

## 5. Review and Verification

### 5.1 Automated Verification
Submitted documents are reviewed using AI-assisted verification to:
- Confirm document authenticity and readability
- Verify that the document supports the requested name change
- Extract the confirmed new name

### 5.2 Manual Review
Requests that cannot be auto-approved will be routed to an HR specialist within 1 business day.

### 5.3 Decision Timeframes
| Step | Target Timeframe |
|------|------------------|
| Acknowledgment | Immediate (automated) |
| Automated review | Within 1 hour |
| Manual review (if needed) | Within 1 business day |
| HR approval | Within 2 business days |
| Systems update | Within 5 business days of approval |

## 6. What Gets Updated

Upon approval, the following systems will be updated:
- **Workday (HRIS):** Legal name, display name
- **Active Directory / Identity Provider:** Login name (if applicable)
- **Email address:** New alias added; old email forwarded for 90 days
- **Benefits systems:** Carrier notifications sent
- **Payroll:** W-2 and payroll records
- **Badge / Physical Access:** New badge issued within 5 business days
- **Company directory:** Updated within 1 business day of system update

You will receive a notification when each system has been updated.

## 7. Employee Responsibilities

- Ensure your legal name change has been processed by the Social Security Administration (SSA) before or concurrent with this request
- Update your Social Security card name before updating tax records
- Notify your bank, insurance carriers, and other personal accounts separately — the company update does not cover personal accounts
- Update any professional licenses or certifications that may require a separate name change process

## 8. Privacy and Confidentiality

Name change requests are treated with strict confidentiality:
- Accessible only to the employee, their HR Business Partner, and authorized HR administrators
- Reason for the name change (e.g., marriage, divorce, transition) is not shared with managers or colleagues without employee consent
- Employees are not required to explain the reason for the name change

Colleagues will be notified of the name update in the company directory; they will not be told the reason for the change.

## 9. Rejection and Appeals

Requests may be rejected if:
- Documentation is not legible or does not clearly support the name change
- The document type provided does not match the request

If rejected:
- You will receive an email explaining the reason
- You may resubmit with corrected documentation at any time
- You may appeal by contacting hr@company.com or your HR Business Partner directly
