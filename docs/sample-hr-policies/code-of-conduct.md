# Code of Conduct

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources & Legal
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Introduction

Our Code of Conduct reflects our commitment to integrity, respect, and excellence. Every member of our organization is expected to uphold these standards in all professional interactions.

## 2. Core Values

### 2.1 Integrity
- Act honestly and transparently in all business dealings
- Never misrepresent information to colleagues, customers, or partners
- Acknowledge mistakes and take responsibility for correcting them
- Protect confidential information appropriately

### 2.2 Respect
- Treat all individuals with dignity, regardless of their background or role
- Value diverse perspectives and foster an inclusive environment
- Communicate professionally and constructively
- Resolve conflicts through dialogue and mutual understanding

### 2.3 Excellence
- Deliver high-quality work and strive for continuous improvement
- Meet commitments and communicate proactively when challenges arise
- Support colleagues and share knowledge freely
- Pursue professional development opportunities

## 3. Workplace Behavior

### 3.1 Professional Standards
Employees are expected to:
- Arrive on time and fulfill job responsibilities reliably
- Maintain professional appearance appropriate to the work environment
- Use company resources (technology, equipment, time) responsibly and for legitimate business purposes
- Avoid conflicts of interest or disclose them promptly when they arise

### 3.2 Prohibited Conduct
The following behaviors are strictly prohibited:
- Discrimination based on protected characteristics (race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, etc.)
- Harassment of any kind (sexual, racial, religious, etc.)
- Violence, threats, or intimidation
- Theft, fraud, or misappropriation of company resources
- Falsification of records or reports
- Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information
- Substance abuse in the workplace
- Violation of applicable laws and regulations

## 4. Conflicts of Interest

### 4.1 Definition
A conflict of interest exists when personal interests may interfere with acting in the best interests of the company or its stakeholders.

### 4.2 Disclosure Requirements
Employees must disclose:
- Financial interests in competitors, suppliers, or customers
- Personal relationships with individuals in a reporting relationship
- Outside employment or consulting arrangements
- Receipt of gifts above a nominal value ($25)

### 4.3 Process
Disclose potential conflicts to your manager and HR in writing. HR will assess and determine appropriate mitigation steps.

## 5. Confidentiality and Data Protection

- Protect confidential business information from unauthorized disclosure
- Follow data classification and handling procedures
- Report data breaches or security incidents immediately to IT Security
- Comply with all applicable privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)

## 6. Use of Technology

- Company systems are for business use; incidental personal use is permitted if it does not interfere with work
- Do not install unauthorized software or access restricted systems
- Protect credentials and never share passwords
- Do not engage in activities that could expose the company to legal risk (piracy, inappropriate content, etc.)

## 7. Social Media

- Clearly identify personal opinions as your own, not the company's position
- Do not share confidential company information on social platforms
- Be respectful and professional when discussing the company publicly
- Follow brand guidelines when representing the company officially

## 8. Reporting Violations

### 8.1 Channels
- **Manager:** First point of contact for minor concerns
- **HR Department:** hr@company.com or extension 5000
- **Ethics Hotline:** 1-800-XXX-XXXX (anonymous, 24/7)
- **Online Portal:** ethics.company.com

### 8.2 Non-Retaliation
Retaliation against good-faith reporters is strictly prohibited and will result in disciplinary action.

## 9. Consequences of Violations

Violations will be addressed through a range of responses based on severity:
- Coaching or counseling
- Written warning
- Performance improvement plan
- Suspension
- Termination
- Legal action where required

## 10. Acknowledgment

All employees must acknowledge receipt and understanding of this Code of Conduct annually, typically during the performance review cycle.
