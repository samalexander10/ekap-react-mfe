# PTO and Leave Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Paid Time Off (PTO)

### 1.1 Accrual Rates

PTO accrues based on years of continuous service:

| Years of Service | Annual PTO Days | Accrual Rate (per pay period) |
|-----------------|-----------------|-------------------------------|
| 0–1 year        | 15 days         | 0.577 days                    |
| 1–3 years       | 18 days         | 0.692 days                    |
| 3–7 years       | 22 days         | 0.846 days                    |
| 7+ years        | 25 days         | 0.962 days                    |

### 1.2 PTO Usage
- PTO can be used for vacation, personal days, and non-work-related illness
- PTO must be approved by your manager in advance (except for unexpected illness)
- Minimum increment: Half-day (4 hours)
- Requests should be submitted at least 2 weeks in advance for absences of 3+ days

### 1.3 PTO Carryover
- Maximum carryover: 10 days into the next calendar year
- Unused PTO above the carryover limit is forfeited on January 1
- Employees are encouraged to use PTO throughout the year

### 1.4 PTO Payout
- Upon termination (voluntary or involuntary), accrued and unused PTO up to the carryover cap will be paid out
- PTO payout is calculated at your base rate of pay at termination

## 2. Holidays

The company observes the following paid holidays:

1. New Year's Day (January 1)
2. Martin Luther King Jr. Day (third Monday in January)
3. Presidents' Day (third Monday in February)
4. Memorial Day (last Monday in May)
5. Juneteenth (June 19)
6. Independence Day (July 4)
7. Labor Day (first Monday in September)
8. Thanksgiving Day (fourth Thursday in November)
9. Day after Thanksgiving (fourth Friday in November)
10. Christmas Eve (December 24)
11. Christmas Day (December 25)

When a holiday falls on a Saturday, it is observed on Friday. When a holiday falls on a Sunday, it is observed on Monday.

### 2.1 Floating Holidays
Employees receive 2 floating holidays per calendar year to use at their discretion, with manager approval.

## 3. Sick Leave

### 3.1 Sick Leave Accrual
- All employees accrue 1 hour of sick leave for every 30 hours worked
- Maximum accrual: 80 hours (10 days) per year
- Unused sick leave carries over up to a maximum of 80 hours

### 3.2 Sick Leave Usage
Sick leave may be used for:
- Employee's own illness, injury, or medical appointment
- Caring for an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, sibling)
- Mental health days
- Domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking (safe leave)

### 3.3 Documentation
For absences of 3 or more consecutive days, a physician's note or other documentation may be required upon return.

## 4. Parental Leave

### 4.1 Eligibility
Employees who have been employed for at least 12 months and worked 1,250 hours in the past 12 months.

### 4.2 Primary Caregiver Leave
- 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave
- Available for: birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child
- Must be taken within 12 months of the qualifying event
- Can be taken continuously or intermittently (with HR approval)

### 4.3 Secondary Caregiver Leave
- 4 weeks of fully paid leave
- Same eligibility and timing requirements as primary caregiver leave

### 4.4 Coordination with State Leave
Company leave runs concurrently with any applicable state-mandated family leave.

## 5. Medical Leave (FMLA)

Employees eligible under the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) are entitled to:
- Up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave per year
- Up to 26 weeks of unpaid leave to care for a covered servicemember

### 5.1 FMLA Qualifying Reasons
- Serious health condition (employee or immediate family member)
- Birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child
- Qualifying military exigency

### 5.2 FMLA Process
1. Notify HR and your manager as soon as possible
2. Complete FMLA paperwork within 15 days
3. Provide medical certification from a healthcare provider
4. HR will designate leave as FMLA-qualifying

## 6. Bereavement Leave

| Relationship         | Paid Leave Days |
|---------------------|-----------------|
| Spouse/domestic partner | 5 days       |
| Child (including stepchild, foster, adopted) | 5 days |
| Parent (including stepparent, in-law) | 5 days |
| Sibling (including step, half) | 3 days |
| Grandparent/grandchild | 3 days        |
| Close friend or extended family | 1 day  |

Additional unpaid bereavement leave may be approved at manager discretion.

## 7. Jury Duty

- Employees are fully paid during jury duty service up to 10 business days
- For extended jury service beyond 10 days, the company will supplement to ensure you receive full pay
- Provide jury summons to your manager and HR promptly
- If released from jury duty for a full day, employees are expected to return to work

## 8. Military Leave

The company complies with the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA):
- Up to 2 weeks fully paid military leave per year
- Extended unpaid leave as required by law
- Health benefits continuation for up to 24 months
- Reemployment rights upon return

## 9. Requesting Leave

All leave requests are managed through the HR system at hr.company.com/leave:
1. Log in and select "Request Leave"
2. Choose leave type and dates
3. Submit for manager approval
4. HR will confirm leave designation and any documentation requirements

For medical or sensitive leave matters, contact HR directly at leave@company.com.
