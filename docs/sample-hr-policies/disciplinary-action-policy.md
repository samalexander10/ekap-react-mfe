# Disciplinary Action Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy provides a fair and consistent framework for addressing employee performance issues and conduct violations. The goal is to help employees improve and succeed, while also protecting the organization and its people.

## 2. Philosophy

We believe in:
- **Fairness:** Every employee deserves to understand expectations and have an opportunity to improve
- **Consistency:** Similar situations are handled similarly, without favoritism or bias
- **Progressiveness:** In most cases, escalating steps are used before termination
- **Documentation:** All disciplinary actions are documented in writing

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to all employees. Contractors and temporary workers are subject to the terms of their specific agreements.

## 4. Progressive Discipline Steps

### Step 1: Verbal Warning (Counseling)
- Manager addresses the issue in a private meeting
- Specific concern, expected behavior, and consequences of non-improvement are communicated
- Manager documents the conversation in the HR system within 24 hours
- Employee may provide a written response

### Step 2: Written Warning
- Formal written notice detailing:
  - The specific performance or conduct issue
  - Prior counseling (if applicable)
  - Specific expectations going forward
  - Timeline for improvement
  - Consequences of continued non-compliance
- Employee signs to acknowledge receipt (signature does not imply agreement)
- Copy filed in employee's personnel record
- HR Business Partner is notified

### Step 3: Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) or Final Written Warning
- Used for recurring issues or more serious violations
- **PIP** includes: specific, measurable goals; timeline (typically 30–90 days); support resources; check-in schedule
- **Final Written Warning** explicitly states that further violations will result in termination
- HR Business Partner and manager co-develop the PIP/Final Warning
- Employee meets with manager and HR to review the plan

### Step 4: Suspension (with or without pay)
- May be used while an investigation is ongoing or as a disciplinary measure
- **Suspension with pay:** Typically used during investigations involving serious allegations
- **Suspension without pay:** Used as a disciplinary step for exempt employees only in full-week increments to comply with FLSA; non-exempt employees may be suspended in shorter increments
- Duration: 1–5 business days typically; longer suspensions require HR Director approval

### Step 5: Termination
- The final step in the progressive discipline process, or immediate in cases of gross misconduct
- Requires HR Director sign-off prior to notification to employee
- Manager and HR conduct the termination meeting
- Final pay, benefits continuation (COBRA), and return of company property are addressed

## 5. Grounds for Immediate Termination

Certain actions are so serious that they warrant immediate termination without progressive steps:
- Theft, embezzlement, or fraud
- Physical violence or threats of violence
- Sexual harassment or assault
- Discrimination or creation of a hostile work environment
- Serious safety violations
- Unauthorized disclosure of trade secrets or confidential information
- Drug or alcohol impairment at work
- Falsification of company records
- Insubordination of a serious nature
- Criminal conviction directly related to job duties

## 6. Roles and Responsibilities

### Manager
- Identify and address performance or conduct concerns promptly
- Consult with HR before initiating formal disciplinary action
- Deliver disciplinary discussions in private, professional settings
- Document all steps accurately and timely

### HR Business Partner
- Advise managers on appropriate disciplinary steps
- Ensure consistency and legal compliance
- Participate in Step 3 and beyond
- Maintain documentation in HR systems

### Employee
- Right to be informed of concerns in writing (Step 2 and beyond)
- Right to provide a written response to disciplinary documentation
- Right to request HR involvement at any stage
- Right to appeal decisions (see Section 8)

## 7. Documentation

All disciplinary actions are documented and retained:
- Verbal warnings: Manager's notes in HR system
- Written warnings, PIPs, Final Warnings: Signed copies filed in personnel record
- Termination: Documented with reason, final pay calculations, and exit process
- Records retained per applicable law (minimum 5 years)

## 8. Appeals Process

Employees may appeal formal disciplinary actions (Step 2 and above):
1. Submit written appeal to HR Director within 5 business days of receiving the action
2. HR Director reviews with relevant parties and responds within 10 business days
3. Decision of HR Director is final, except where an investigation reveals procedural error or new information

Appeals do not apply to at-will employment terminations, except where a claim of discrimination, retaliation, or policy violation is raised.

## 9. At-Will Employment

Nothing in this policy alters the at-will nature of employment. The company reserves the right to skip progressive discipline steps or terminate employment at any time, with or without cause or notice, except as prohibited by law.
