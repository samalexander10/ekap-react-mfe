# Sexual Harassment Prevention Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes our commitment to a workplace free from sexual harassment. All employees, contractors, vendors, and visitors are expected to maintain a professional and respectful environment.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees (full-time, part-time, temporary), contractors, interns, and anyone conducting business on behalf of the organization, regardless of their position or level.

## 3. Definition of Sexual Harassment

Sexual harassment is unwelcome conduct of a sexual nature that:
- Affects an individual's employment
- Unreasonably interferes with work performance
- Creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment

### 3.1 Types of Sexual Harassment

**Quid Pro Quo:** When submission to or rejection of sexual conduct is used as a basis for employment decisions.

**Hostile Work Environment:** When sexual conduct is sufficiently severe or pervasive to alter the conditions of employment.

### 3.2 Examples of Prohibited Conduct

- Unwanted sexual advances or propositions
- Requests for sexual favors
- Sexually explicit jokes, comments, or innuendo
- Displaying or sharing sexually suggestive images, videos, or materials
- Unwanted physical contact (touching, grabbing, blocking movement)
- Leering or staring in a sexual manner
- Making sexual comments about someone's body or clothing
- Sending sexually explicit emails, texts, or messages
- Commenting on someone's romantic or sexual life without invitation

## 4. Reporting Procedures

### 4.1 How to Report

Employees who experience or witness sexual harassment should report it through any of these channels:

1. **Direct Manager:** Report to your direct supervisor (unless they are the alleged harasser)
2. **HR Department:** Contact HR directly at hr@company.com or extension 5000
3. **Anonymous Hotline:** Call 1-800-XXX-XXXX (available 24/7)
4. **Online Reporting Portal:** ethics.company.com/report

### 4.2 What to Document

When reporting, document:
- Date, time, and location of the incident(s)
- Name(s) of the alleged harasser(s)
- Description of the conduct
- Names of any witnesses
- How the conduct affected your work

## 5. Investigation Process

All reports will be:
1. Acknowledged within 24 hours
2. Investigated promptly, thoroughly, and impartially
3. Kept as confidential as possible while allowing for proper investigation
4. Resolved with appropriate action within 30 business days when possible

## 6. Non-Retaliation

Retaliation against anyone who reports sexual harassment, participates in an investigation, or opposes prohibited conduct is strictly prohibited and will itself be subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination.

## 7. Consequences

Violations of this policy may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Mandatory training
- Suspension with or without pay
- Demotion
- Termination of employment
- Legal referral where appropriate

## 8. Training Requirements

- All employees must complete mandatory anti-harassment training within 30 days of hire
- Annual refresher training is required for all staff
- Managers receive additional training on investigation procedures and responsibilities
